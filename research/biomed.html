Biomedical device modeling, design, simulation and validation

Implantable medical devices like pacemakers and defibrillators are very complex systems. An implantable device today can have up to 100,000 lines of software, and heterogeneous hardware components like processors, PCB, electrode arrays, ICs, actuators, sensors and controllers. According to a recent study on the total number of recalls and adverse events of computer based medical devices reported between 2006-2011 to the FDA, 67% of the computer related recalls are due to software failures. Another study reports that hardware related recalls have higher impact than software related issues in terms of criticality and account for nearly 57% of the recalls in the market. In this time period, the number of safety-critical medical devices with severe life threatening consequences to patients recalled were 2,447,894 with 95% of them relating to software/hardware failures. The responsibility to test, validate and verify the device software to demonstrate its safety and efficacy rests solely on the manufacturer.

We are interested in the development of computational models and algorithms that can model the hardware, software, , sensors, actuators and physical interactions between the patient and the device. In particular, we are interested in pacemakers, hearing aids and trauma monitoring devices.
